Our Story

A school built on love, rigour, and the long view.

We started small in Parkview Estate, Ikoyi — and we have stayed small on purpose. Every child here is known. Every parent is in conversation. That is the Le Poshe way.

— 01 / Origin

From a single classroom to a beloved Ikoyi institution.

Le Poshe was founded by an educator and mother who could not find the kind of school she wanted for her own children — small, calm, intellectually rich, and deeply caring.

So she built one. From a single Parkview classroom to a full nursery and primary school, every choice we make is shaped by that founding question: "Would I happily send my own child here?"

The answer, every day, is yes.

A child who is seen learns to see. A child who is heard learns to listen. A child who is loved learns to love. Everything begins there.

— Le Poshe Founding Principles
— 02 / Values

Four principles. Lived daily.

These are not posters on a wall. They shape how our teachers plan lessons, how we speak to children, and how parents experience us every morning.

I

Individualised

No child is a number. We adjust pace, support, and challenge to each learner — every term, every year.

R

Rigorous

Joyful classrooms, high standards. We expect the best — and equip our pupils to deliver it.

R

Rooted

Proudly Nigerian. We celebrate language, culture, and heritage as much as we teach Maths.

W

Warm

Calm voices. Patient teachers. A school children run into, not away from.

— 03 / Leadership

A school led by an educator, not an administrator.

Our proprietress is in the school every day — meeting parents, observing classes, and shaping the culture from the inside.

Behind her stands a team of trained, certified teachers whose average tenure with us exceeds five years. Continuity, in a child's primary years, is everything.
